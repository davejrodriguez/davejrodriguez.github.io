The Virtual Middletown Living Museum project in Blue Mars is a simulation of the Ball Glass factory from early 20th century Muncie, Indiana. Life and conditions in the factory were one of the key elements of the Middletown Studies\u00a0by Robert S. and Helen Merrell Lynd in their landmark studies\u00a0Middletown<\/em>\u00a0(1929) and\u00a0Middletown in Transition<\/em>\u00a0(1937). These in-depth accounts of life in Muncie, Indiana, became classic sociological studies and established the community as a barometer of social trends in the United States.\u00a0In the years since, scholars in a variety of fields have returned to Muncie to follow up on the Lynds\u2019 work, making this\u00a0small city among the most studied communities in the nation. The center continues this tradition by sponsoring and promoting\u00a0research<\/a>\u00a0on Muncie as Middletown, on\u00a0small cities<\/a>\u00a0generally, and on the themes and issues the Lynds explored.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
